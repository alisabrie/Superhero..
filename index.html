Spider Man

Spider-Man, created by brStan Lee
and Steve Ditko in 1962, is the
alter ego of Peter Parker, a young
man who gains spider-like powers
after being bitten by a radioactive
spider. Known for his agility,
wall-crawling abilities, and
"spider-sense," Peter uses his
intellect to create web-shooters
and fight crime. Guided by the
principle "With great power comes
great responsibility," Spider-Man
balances his superhero duties with
the challenges of everyday life.

Super Man

Superman, created by Jerry Siegel
and Joe Shuster in 1938, is the
alter ego of Clark Kent, an alien
from Krypton sent to Earth.
Raised by the Kent family, Clark
discovers his superhuman abilities,
including strength, flight, and
invulnerability. As Superman, he
becomes a symbol of hope and
justice, using his powers to protect
humanity from various threats,
always guided by a strong moral
compass.

Iron Man

Iron Man, created by Stan Lee,
Larry Lieber, Don Heck, and
Jack Kirby in 1963, is the alter
ego of Tony Stark, a genius
inventor and billionaire. After
being injured and captured,
Tony builds a high-tech suit of
armor to escape, marking the
start of his career as Iron Man.
With his advanced suits equipped
with powerful weapons and
technology, Iron Man fights
global threats and is a founding
member of the Avengers.

Captain America

Captain America, created by
Joe Simon and Jack Kirby in
1941, is the alter ego of Steve
Rogers, a young man transformed
into a super-soldier during World
War II. With enhanced strength,
agility, and an indestructible
shield, Captain America fights for
freedom and justice. After being
frozen and later revived, he
continues to lead the Avengers,
embodying the ideals of bravery
and integrity.
